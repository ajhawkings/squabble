Tips for speaking

  • Stay on topic: You have limited time to be convincing, so don't waste valuable seconds on something off-topic
  • Speak clearly and don't rush: Many new debaters mumble or rush what they're saying — don't fall into this trap
  • Be confident: No-one can be perfect at something they're new to, so don't be afraid to to take risks and put yourself out there
  • Think about your body language: Speaking is about selling your viewpoint to the audience, so body language can have a very large impact
  • Listen: To be a good speaker, it's important to have a good understanding of counterarguments that invalidate your stance — so make sure you are respectful and listen carefully to the other players
  • Anticipate your opponent's questions: This can be a very effective tactic because it allows you extra time to come up with convincing arguments, and you can use this to make your opponents' views seem less valid
  • Take breaks: Stop and take a breath when needed - don't be afraid to pause and gather your thoughts.
  • Have fun: Remember, this is a game! It's designed to both improve your communication skills AND allow you to enjoy yourself
